About the organization

Project Oz supports youth and young adults across McLean and Livingston counties.

Project Oz is an Illinois-based nonprofit located in Bloomington, Illinois. It provides crisis intervention, housing support, mental health services, emergency shelter, transitional housing, substance use prevention, and school-based support.

Since 1973 Project Oz has helped youth and families build safer, healthier paths forward.
8,000 youth Project Oz reports reaching about 8,000 youth every year.
400+ youth Project Oz reports helping more than 400 runaway and homeless youth every year.

Who they support

Project Oz supports youth and young adults, typically ages 10–23, who may be experiencing homelessness, family conflict, mental health challenges, substance use concerns, or other crisis situations. The organization also works with families to strengthen relationships and prevent future problems.

The local issue

Youth homelessness, family instability, and mental health challenges continue to affect young people in Bloomington-Normal and the surrounding communities. Project Oz helps meet these needs through emergency shelter, counseling, outreach services, school support, and housing assistance.

Mission and values

Project Oz believes every young person deserves safety, opportunity, and well-being. The organization focuses on positive relationships, homelessness prevention, mental health support, family stability, education, and independence.

Services

How Project Oz fulfills its mission

Emergency shelter

Temporary shelter and crisis response for youth who have run away or do not have a safe place to stay.

Housing assistance

Transitional housing, supportive services, and help finding a safer path out of homelessness.

Counseling and mental health

Counseling, safety planning, mental health education, and support for youth and families.

School-based support

Support in Unit 5 and District 87 schools to help students with school, family, peer conflict, and personal challenges.

Life skills classes

Classes that teach budgeting, housing readiness, job preparation, nutrition, self-care, and safety information.

Substance use prevention

Education and prevention programs focused on alcohol, tobacco, and other drug use.
